Cairn is an AI-driven automation system that autonomously plans, executes, and adapts complex security assessments and problem-solving tasks. Powered by large language models with dynamic tool use, Cairn demonstrated its capabilities by being the **sole team to achieve a full score (AK)** in the Tencent Cloud 2nd AI Penetration Challenge — placing 4th overall in the online competition.

## What Cairn does

Cairn combines a large language model reasoning engine with a dynamic tool-use framework to autonomously solve multi-step problems. In the security domain, this means Cairn can plan an attack surface, identify vulnerabilities, chain exploits, and report findings — all without manual intervention.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install and configure">
    Set up Cairn with your LLM provider credentials and target configuration. See the [installation guide](/installation) for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define your task">
    Describe the problem or assessment goal in natural language. Cairn's planner breaks it into executable sub-tasks automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run and monitor">
    Cairn autonomously selects tools, executes steps, and adapts its strategy based on intermediate results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review results">
    Collect structured findings with reasoning traces, tool outputs, and actionable recommendations.
  </Step>
</Steps>
