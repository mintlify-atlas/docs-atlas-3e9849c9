Cairn is released under the [GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0)](https://github.com/oritera/Cairn/blob/main/LICENSE). This page explains what that means in plain terms, who needs a commercial license, and how contributions are handled.

## What AGPL-3.0 means for you

AGPL-3.0 is a strong copyleft open source license. It gives you broad rights to use, study, modify, and distribute Cairn — with one key condition that goes further than the standard GPL.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use freely" icon="circle-check">
    Run Cairn for any purpose: personal projects, research, education, internal security assessments, or CTF competitions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Modify the code" icon="code">
    Read, edit, and adapt the source to your needs. You are not required to publish modifications that you use privately.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Distribute copies" icon="share-nodes">
    Share Cairn or your modified version with others, provided you include the AGPL-3.0 license and make the full corresponding source available.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Network use triggers sharing" icon="network-wired">
    If you run a modified version of Cairn as a service that others interact with over a network, you must make your modified source code available to those users. This is the key AGPL addition over standard GPL.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  The AGPL-3.0 network provision applies to modified versions. If you run an unmodified copy of Cairn as a network service, you still need to comply with the license terms, but you are not required to publish any additional source since the original source is already public.
</Note>

## Personal and educational use

Personal and educational use of Cairn is free under AGPL-3.0. This covers:

- Individual security researchers and penetration testers using Cairn on systems they are authorized to test
- Students and academics studying AI-driven security automation
- CTF competitors using Cairn in competition environments that permit automated tooling
- Hobbyists exploring autonomous agent systems

No registration, payment, or additional agreement is required for these use cases.

## Commercial use

AGPL-3.0 permits commercial use. You can use Cairn in a commercial context without a separate license as long as you comply with all AGPL-3.0 obligations — most importantly, the source-sharing requirement for network services.

A commercial license is required (or strongly recommended) if:

- You want to incorporate Cairn into a proprietary product and distribute it without making your modifications public
- You are building a SaaS or managed service on top of Cairn and the AGPL-3.0 network provision is incompatible with your business model
- You need contractual warranty, indemnification, or support terms that the AGPL-3.0 does not provide
- Your legal or compliance team requires a non-copyleft license for your software stack

<Warning>
  If you are uncertain whether your use case requires a commercial license, consult your legal team before shipping. The AGPL-3.0 network provision is broader than most developers expect.
</Warning>

## How to request a commercial license

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your use case">
    Confirm that your intended use falls outside what AGPL-3.0 permits without a commercial agreement. The FAQ question ["Can I use Cairn commercially?"](/reference/faq) covers common scenarios.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open an issue or contact the maintainer">
    Open an issue on the [Cairn GitHub repository](https://github.com/oritera/Cairn) with the subject "Commercial license inquiry" and a brief description of your intended use. Alternatively, contact the maintainer directly using the contact information on the repository profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Discuss terms">
    The maintainer will respond to discuss your use case and the terms of a commercial license. Commercial licensing is handled individually to accommodate varying needs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Contributions

Cairn welcomes contributions from the community. Before submitting a pull request, note the following:

By submitting a contribution to the Cairn repository, you agree that:

- Your contribution may be used under the AGPL-3.0 license, the same as the rest of the project.
- The maintainer may also include your contribution in commercially licensed versions of Cairn.

This dual-use arrangement (sometimes called a Contributor License Agreement, or CLA) allows the project to remain open source while also making a commercial license offering viable. You retain copyright over your own contributions.

<Info>
  If you have concerns about the contribution terms or need to discuss licensing your contributions under different conditions, open an issue on the repository before submitting your pull request.
</Info>
